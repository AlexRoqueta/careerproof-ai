Moderate–high exposure

Several core tasks in this role are highly automatable with current AI tools, but durable human-judgment work keeps the role valuable when paired with the right skills.

Tasks most exposed to AI

  • Routine variance reporting — recurring spreadsheet roll-ups and commentary are increasingly drafted by AI.
  • Data cleaning and reconciliation — pattern-heavy work that automation handles reliably.
  • First-draft financial summaries — narrative drafts AI can produce in seconds for a human to refine.

Your durable human advantages

  • Framing the right questions for ambiguous, high-stakes decisions.
  • Stakeholder trust and judgment that AI output still has to pass through.
  • Connecting numbers to strategy in ways that require context AI does not have.

Skills to build next

  • AI-assisted financial modeling and prompt design for analysis workflows.
  • Data storytelling and executive communication.
  • Scenario planning and decision frameworks that compound with experience.

Your 30 / 60 / 90-day plan

  1. Days 1–30: Adopt one AI tool for routine reporting and reclaim time for higher-judgment work.
  2. Days 31–60: Lead one AI-assisted analysis project end to end and document the workflow.
  3. Days 61–90: Position yourself as the person who pairs AI leverage with financial judgment on your team.

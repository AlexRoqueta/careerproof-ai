The honest answer: it depends on your tasks, not your title

Almost every job is a bundle of tasks. Some — writing routine summaries, drafting first-pass copy, structuring messy text into clean spreadsheets, classifying support tickets — are highly exposed to today's AI. Others — building relationships, owning ambiguous outcomes, navigating organizations, judgment under uncertainty — are far less exposed.

That's why "will AI replace my job?" is the wrong frame. The better frame is: which tasks in my role are most exposed, what does that mean for me in the next 12 months, and what should I be learning right now?

What it isn't

We're not going to tell you your job is "safe" — and we're not going to tell you to panic. The report is directional career-risk intelligence. It surfaces signal you can plan around, and it's transparent about what it can't predict (the economy, your team's strategy, your company's roadmap, the next year of AI capability jumps).

The goal isn't to predict the future — it's to make sure the next 90 days of your career are spent on the things that compound.

How to use the report well

  1. Read the score, then ignore it. The number is a quick gut-check. The task breakdown is where the real information is.
  2. Highlight the 3 most exposed tasks. Pick one to delegate to AI tools and one to step away from over the next quarter.
  3. Pick 2 skills to build. Not 8. Two — and start them this month.
  4. Calendar the 30/60/90 plan. Even loosely. The point is to make the next move easy.
